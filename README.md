Meld is an open-source Android music client designed to give you the best of both worlds. It leverages your Spotify account to provide personalized recommendations, home feeds, and search, while streaming audio seamlessly through YouTube Music’s extensive library—all without needing a Spotify Premium subscription.

## 💡 The Problem
Many music lovers find themselves caught between two worlds: the superior recommendation engine and social discovery features of Spotify, and the comprehensive, free-tier-friendly music library of YouTube Music. Meld bridges this gap, allowing you to enjoy your Spotify-curated taste profile while accessing the deep catalog and high-quality streaming capabilities of YouTube Music.

## ✨ Key Features
*   **Unified Experience:** Spotify for discovery and home feeds; YouTube Music for high-quality streaming.
*   **No Premium Required:** Use your free Spotify account to power recommendations and library management.
*   **Smart Matching:** Intelligent, fuzzy-match algorithm that maps Spotify tracks to the correct YouTube Music videos.
*   **Personalized Queues:** A custom recommendation engine built on your Spotify listening history.
*   **Rich Playback Features:**
    *   Background playback
    *   Offline downloads
    *   Live, real-time lyrics
    *   10-band parametric equalizer for custom audio tuning
*   **User-Centric Controls:** Full playlist management, audio normalization, sleep timers, and Material 3 design with dynamic themes.

## ⚙️ How It Works
Meld works as a client-side bridge. You authenticate with your Spotify account within the app (via secure WebView), allowing Meld to sync your top tracks, playlists, and artists.

When you play a track, Meld’s engine:
1.  **Analyzes** the Spotify metadata.
2.  **Matches** that track to the corresponding official video on YouTube Music.
3.  **Streams** the audio directly through YouTube’s infrastructure, ensuring you get the performance and features you expect.

## 📥 Installation
Meld is available for Android 8.0 and higher.

1.  Visit the [GitHub Releases Page](https://github.com/FrancescoGrazioso/Meld/releases).
2.  Download the latest `Meld.apk` file.
3.  Open the file on your device and follow the installation prompts. 
    > **Note:** You may need to enable *Install from Unknown Sources* in your Android settings.
